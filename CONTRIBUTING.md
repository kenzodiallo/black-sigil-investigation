# Contributing to Black Sigil Investigation

Thank you for your interest in contributing to **Black Sigil Investigation**.

This repository serves as a **collection and staging hub** for open-source intelligence (OSINT), references, notes and preliminary analysis used to support investigative and analytical work.

All contributions are reviewed manually.  
Nothing is published automatically.

---

## Scope of Contributions

You may contribute:

- Open-source intelligence (OSINT)
- Publicly available documents and references
- Analytical notes and contextual explanations
- Links to verifiable public sources
- Raw data intended for further verification

---

## What is NOT accepted

Contributions must **NOT** include:

- Private or personal data (non-public individuals)
- Leaked, classified or illegally obtained material
- Doxxing, harassment or targeted accusations
- Content based on rumors or unverifiable claims
- Calls for violence or illegal activity

Submissions violating these rules will be rejected without notice.

---

## How to Contribute via GitHub

### 1. Fork the repository

Create your own fork of this repository.

### 2. Add your contribution

Depending on the content type:

- `_posts/`  
  For short analytical notes, research logs or OSINT observations  
  Format: Markdown (`.md`)

- `collecte/`  
  For raw data, documents, source collections or reference material

Please include:
- A clear title
- Date
- Source(s)
- Context or explanation of relevance

### 3. Submit a Pull Request

- Use a descriptive title
- Explain what the contribution contains
- Mention sources and verification status

---

## Alternative Submission (Non-GitHub)

If you are not comfortable using GitHub, you may submit information via secure email.

Instructions are available on the project website.

---

## Review Process

- All submissions are reviewed manually
- Acceptance does not imply publication
- Content may be edited, summarized or discarded
- No response is guaranteed

---

## Disclaimer

Black Sigil Investigation operates strictly within the limits of:
- Open-source intelligence
- Public information
- Ethical research practices

By contributing, you confirm that your submission complies with these principles.

---

Thank you for contributing responsibly.
